Negative Effects of Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying might seem harmless at first, but it can cause serious damage.
Young people between 15 and 20 years old often feel these effects the most, since they spen a lot of time online.

Mental Health Problems

Cyberbullying can make someone feel sad, scared, or stressed.
Many young people lose self-confidence and start believing they are not good enough.
Some fall into depression or have trouble sleeping because of the hurtful messages they receive.

Social Consequences

People who are bullied often isolate themselves.
They talk less with friends, delete their social media, or become afraid to interact with others.

Problems at School or Work

Because of stress and fear, victims have trouble concentrating.
Their grades drop, and they often lose motivation to study or work.

Low Self-Esteem

Cyberbullying damages self-esteem.
Victims start doubting themselves and believing what bullies say about them.

Severe Emotional Impact

In serious cases, cyberbullying can lead to self-harm or even suicidal thoughts.
What may seem like a joke to one person can be life-threatening to another.

Damage to the Online Reputation

What's posted online often stays there for a long time.
Photos, videos, or messages can keep spreading, even after they are deleted.

Why Cyberbullying is Especially Harmful to Teens

The unique vulnerabilities of ages 15-20 make online harassment particularly devastating.

Developing Brain vulnerability

Teen brains are still developing emotional regulation, making them more sensitive to online attacks.

Peer Acceptance Crisis

Online Rejection can shatter self-worth during this critical identity formation period.

Digital Native Dependenc?

Teens rely heavily on digital communication, making online bullying feel all-encompassing.

Critical Life Transitions

College, career, and relationships can all be affected by cyberbullying trauma.
