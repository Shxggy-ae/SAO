MISINFORMATION ALERT

FAKE NEWS: THE DIGITAL PLAGUE

Not everything you see online is true. Fake news spreads faster than ever and can cause real-world harm. Learn to spot manipulation, disinformation, and propaganda before it's too late.

Why It's Dangerous

Fake news can incite violence, influence elections, cause panic, and destroy reputations. It's often created deliberately to profit, manipulate opinions, or spread hatred.

Viral Lies

False stories spread 6x faster than truth on social media. Before fact-checkers can respond, millions already believe the lie.

AI-Generated Deepfakes

Synthetic videos and images of real people saying/doing things they never did. Almost impossible to detect with the naked eye.

Bot Armies & Echo Chambers

Fake accounts amplify false narratives. Algorithms trap you in bubbles where everyone agrees, reinforcing lies.

Coordinated Disinformation Campaigns

Organized groups deliberately spread lies to manipulate public opinion, elections, and social movements.

How to Spot Fake News

Check the Source

Is it from a reputable news outlet? Look for "About Us" pages, contact info, and author credentials. Sketch sites hide these.

Read Beyond the Headline

Fake news relies on clickbait headlines. The actual article often contradicts its own title or lacks real evidence.

Reverse Image Search

Right-click on images and search Google Images. Fake news often reuses old photos out of context.

Cross-Reference Multiple Sources

Do other trusted outlets report the same story? If only obscure sites are covering it, it's likely false.

Use Fact-Checking Sites

Visit Snopes, FactCheck.org, PolitiFact, or AFP Fact Check to verify controversial claims before sharing.

Watch for Emotional Manipulation

Fake news triggers extreme anger, fear, or outrage. Real news informs. If you feel manipulated, pause and verify.

TIP: When in doubt, DON'T share it. Sharing unverified content makes you part of the problem. Verify first, share second.
